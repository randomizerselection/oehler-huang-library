# Cambridge IGCSE Business 0264

**Syllabus Reference: 2027-2029**

This document is a classroom planning reference based on the official Cambridge IGCSE Business 0264 syllabus for exams in 2027, 2028 and 2029. It is paraphrased for lesson planning and deck creation; use the official PDF as the final authority for exam entries, assessment rules and exact syllabus wording.

## Assessment Overview

| Paper | Duration | Marks | Weighting | Format |
| --- | --- | ---: | ---: | --- |
| Paper 1 | 1 hour 30 minutes | 80 | 50% | Short answer and data response. Four compulsory questions with stimulus material. |
| Paper 2 | 1 hour 30 minutes | 80 | 50% | Case study. Four compulsory questions based on an insert with data, tables, graphs, extracts or advertisements. |

## 5 Financial Information and Decisions

This topic covers why businesses need finance, sources of finance, working capital, cash-flow forecasts, statements of profit or loss, statements of financial position, profitability, liquidity and users of accounts.

| Ref | Syllabus focus | Planning notes / exam-ready wording |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 5.1.1 | Need for business finance | Main reasons include start-up capital, expansion or growth, replacing existing non-current assets, investing in new technology and working capital. Students should distinguish short-term and long-term finance needs. |
| 5.1.1 | Working capital | Working capital is current assets minus current liabilities. It matters because a business needs enough liquid resources to pay day-to-day expenses and short-term debts. |
| 5.1.2 | Internal sources of finance | Include owners' investment, retained profit, sale of unwanted assets and working capital. Teach advantages and disadvantages in relation to the business situation. |
| 5.1.2 | External sources of finance | Include share capital or issuing shares, venture capital, bank overdrafts, leasing, hire purchase, bank loans, trade credit, government grants and crowdfunding. |
| 5.1.2 | Selecting a source of finance | Factors include size of business, legal form, amount required, length of time, existing loans, cost and purpose. Students should recommend and justify an appropriate source for a given situation. |
| 5.2.1 | Cash and cash-flow forecasts | Students need why cash is important, what a cash-flow forecast is, why it is important, and the main features: cash inflow, cash outflow, net cash flow, opening balance and closing balance. |
| 5.2.1 | Cash-flow forecast skills | Students may amend or complete a simple cash-flow forecast and interpret a simple cash-flow forecast. Candidates will not be assessed on constructing a cash-flow forecast from scratch. |
| 5.2.1 | Short-term cash-flow problems | Possible responses include arranging an overdraft, delaying supplier payments, asking customers to pay more quickly and delaying purchase of non-current assets. |
| 5.3.1 | Profit | Profit matters as a reward for risk-taking, a source of finance, a measure of success and a way to attract investors. |
| 5.3.2 | Statement of profit or loss | Main features are revenue, cost of sales, gross profit, expenses and profit. Students make simple calculations and decisions from statements, but are not assessed on constructing full statements. |
| 5.4.1 | Statement of financial position | Main elements include non-current assets, current assets, non-current liabilities, current liabilities and capital employed. Students make simple calculations and decisions from statements, but are not assessed on constructing full statements. |
| 5.5.1 | Profitability | Students calculate and interpret gross profit margin, profit margin and ROCE. |
| 5.5.2 | Liquidity | Students calculate and interpret current ratio and acid test ratio. |
| 5.5.3 | Users of accounts | Internal users include owners, managers and employees. External users include suppliers, government and lenders/banks. Students should explain how users use financial information and limitations of accounts and ratio analysis. |

## Formulas and Ratios

| Area | Formula |
| --- | --- |
| Revenue | Selling price per unit x number of units sold |
| Gross profit | Revenue - cost of sales |
| Profit | Total revenue - total costs, or gross profit - expenses |
| Working capital | Current assets - current liabilities |
| Profit margin | Profit / revenue x 100 |
| Gross profit margin | Gross profit / revenue x 100 |
| ROCE | Profit / capital employed x 100 |
| Current ratio | Current assets : current liabilities, or current assets / current liabilities |
| Acid test ratio | (Current assets - inventory) : current liabilities, or (current assets - inventory) / current liabilities |
