# Investment Analysis: Company Analysis

**Textbook-style Definition Overview**

This file is the shared definition source for the Investment Analysis company-analysis course. It expands the shorter slide vocabulary into complete textbook-style definitions for lesson planning, handouts, textbook chapters, quizzes and assessment design.

Priority source rule: where a course term overlaps with the CFA Program glossary, use the CFA-aligned investment and accounting meaning first, then simplify the wording for Grade 9 classroom use. Where no clear CFA glossary equivalent exists, use the course-specific definition from the company-analysis map. Clear CFA glossary matches are recorded in `references/investment-analysis-cfa-glossary-matches.json`; local textbook source notes are recorded in `references/investment-analysis-textbook-definitions.json`.

Each row contains the course reference, English term, Chinese term support, complete English definition, Chinese definition translation and the main course use. Slide wording may be shortened for display, but handouts and textbook-style materials should preserve the meaning here.

## Unit 1: Market foundations

| Ref | Term | Chinese support | Textbook definition | Chinese definition | Course use |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| U1 L1 | investment analysis | 投资分析 | Investment analysis is the process of using source-dated evidence about an asset, business, return, risk and price before making a justified investment judgement. | 投资分析是指在作出有依据的投资判断之前，使用带有来源日期的证据来分析资产、业务、回报、风险和价格的过程。 | Sets the evidence-before-judgement rule for the whole course. |
| U1 L1 | investment | 投资 | Investment is the act of putting money into an asset with the aim of earning a future return, such as income or capital gain, while accepting the possibility of loss. | 投资是指把资金投入某项资产，以期获得未来回报，例如收入或资本利得，同时接受可能发生损失。 | Separates careful long-run judgement from guessing. |
| U1 L1 | speculation | 投机 | Speculation is an attempt to profit from uncertain price movements, usually relying more on expectations, timing or risk-taking than on complete evidence. | 投机是指试图从不确定的价格变动中获利，通常比完整证据更依赖预期、时机判断或承担风险。 | Builds the course boundary against short-term tips and unsupported guesses. |
| U1 L1 | asset | 资产 | An asset is something with economic value that can be owned or controlled, such as cash, property, a bond, a share or a business resource. | 资产是指具有经济价值、可以被拥有或控制的东西，例如现金、房产、债券、股票或企业资源。 | Gives students the basic category before they compare asset risk. |
| U1 L1 | share | 股票 / 股份 | A share is one unit of ownership in a company, giving the shareholder a claim on part of the company's equity and, depending on the share class, certain rights such as votes or dividends. | 股票或股份是公司所有权的一个单位，使股东对公司部分权益拥有要求权，并且视股票类别而可能享有投票权或股息等权利。 | Defines what students are analysing when they discuss listed companies. |
| U1 L2 | stock exchange | 证券交易所 | A stock exchange is a regulated trading venue where investment instruments such as listed shares, ETFs and bonds can be bought and sold under market rules. | 证券交易所是受监管的交易场所，上市股票、ETF 和债券等投资工具可以在其中按照市场规则买卖。 | Explains why shares need organised market infrastructure. |
| U1 L2 | listing | 上市 | A listing is the formal permission for a company's securities to trade on a stock exchange after the company meets the exchange's requirements. | 上市是指公司证券在满足交易所要求后，获得在证券交易所交易的正式许可。 | Connects companies, exchanges and tradable shares. |
| U1 L2 | stock code | 股票代码 | A stock code is the short market identifier used to find a listed security on an exchange or market-data system. | 股票代码是用于在交易所或市场数据系统中查找某一上市证券的简短市场识别代码。 | Helps students identify the exact security being analysed. |
| U1 L2 | liquidity | 流动性 | Liquidity is the ability to trade an asset quickly, at relatively low cost and in meaningful quantities without causing a large price change. | 流动性是指能够较快、以相对较低成本并以有意义的数量交易某项资产，而不会造成较大价格变动的能力。 | Introduces trading friction and why active markets matter. |
| U1 L3 | quote page | 报价页面 | A quote page is a market-data snapshot showing information such as the latest price, price movement, bid, ask, volume and security identifier for a listed asset. | 报价页面是市场数据快照，显示上市资产的最新价格、价格变动、买价、卖价、成交量和证券识别信息等内容。 | Teaches students to read price evidence without treating it as a full judgement. |
| U1 L3 | volume | 成交量 | Volume is the number of shares or other securities traded during a stated period, such as a trading day. | 成交量是指在某一规定期间内交易的股票或其他证券数量，例如一个交易日内的交易数量。 | Shows how much trading activity sits behind a price movement. |
| U1 L3 | bid | 买价 | The bid is the price at which a buyer, dealer or trader is currently willing to buy a specified quantity of a security. | 买价是买方、交易商或交易员目前愿意以其买入指定数量证券的价格。 | Introduces one side of the market quote. |
| U1 L3 | ask | 卖价 | The ask is the price at which a seller, dealer or trader is currently willing to sell an asset, usually for a specified maximum quantity. | 卖价是卖方、交易商或交易员目前愿意以其卖出资产的价格，通常对应一个指定的最大数量。 | Introduces the other side of the market quote. |
| U1 L4 | voting rights | 投票权 | Voting rights are shareholder rights to vote on certain company decisions, such as electing directors or approving major corporate actions. | 投票权是股东对某些公司决策进行投票的权利，例如选举董事或批准重大公司行动。 | Clarifies that ownership can include decision rights. |
| U1 L4 | ordinary share | 普通股 | An ordinary share is a standard equity share in a company that usually carries voting rights and may receive dividends after higher-priority claims are considered. | 普通股是公司中的标准权益股份，通常带有投票权，并可能在更高优先级的要求权得到考虑后获得股息。 | Distinguishes normal shareholder ownership from other claims. |
| U1 L4 | control | 控制权 | Control is the ability to influence or decide company actions, usually through ownership, voting power, board influence or management authority. | 控制权是影响或决定公司行动的能力，通常来自所有权、投票权、董事会影响力或管理权限。 | Prevents students from thinking one small share controls a company. |
| U1 L5 | evidence log | 证据记录 | An evidence log is a structured record of sources, dates, figures, notes and limitations used to support an investment judgement. | 证据记录是对来源、日期、数据、笔记和局限性进行结构化记录，用来支持投资判断。 | Makes source discipline visible and repeatable. |
| U1 L5 | source date | 来源日期 | A source date is the date on which evidence was published, reported or measured. | 来源日期是证据被发布、报告或测量的日期。 | Helps students judge how current evidence is. |
| U1 L5 | accessed date | 访问日期 | An accessed date is the date on which the analyst viewed or used a source. | 访问日期是分析者查看或使用某一来源的日期。 | Records when changing online evidence was checked. |

## Unit 2: Financial statements

| Ref | Term | Chinese support | Textbook definition | Chinese definition | Course use |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| U2 L6 | revenue | 收入 | Revenue is the amount charged or earned from delivering goods or services in the ordinary activities of a business over a stated period, before expenses are deducted. | 收入是企业在一定期间内通过日常经营活动提供商品或服务而收取或赚取的金额，未扣除费用。 | Starts financial-statement analysis with the top line. |
| U2 L6 | sales growth | 销售增长 | Sales growth is the increase in a company's revenue over time, usually measured as a percentage change between two periods. | 销售增长是公司收入随时间增加的情况，通常用两个期间之间的百分比变化来衡量。 | Lets students compare whether demand or business activity is expanding. |
| U2 L6 | business model | 商业模式 | A business model is the way a company creates value, earns revenue, pays costs and turns customer demand into profit or cash flow. | 商业模式是公司创造价值、赚取收入、支付成本，并把客户需求转化为利润或现金流的方式。 | Links revenue evidence to how the company actually works. |
| U2 L7 | cost of sales | 销售成本 | Cost of sales is the cost directly connected with the goods or services sold in a period; for inventory businesses, it is closely related to cost of goods sold. | 销售成本是与某一期间已售商品或服务直接相关的成本；对存货型企业而言，它与销货成本密切相关。 | Shows why sales alone do not prove strength. |
| U2 L7 | gross profit | 毛利 | Gross profit is revenue or sales minus cost of sales, before operating expenses, financing costs and taxes are deducted. | 毛利是收入或销售额减去销售成本后的金额，尚未扣除营业费用、融资成本和税项。 | Measures profit before operating expenses and financing items. |
| U2 L7 | gross margin | 毛利率 | Gross margin is gross profit divided by revenue and expressed as a percentage, showing how much of each unit of sales remains after direct costs. | 毛利率是毛利除以收入并用百分比表示，显示每一单位销售额在扣除直接成本后还剩下多少。 | Gives students their first profitability percentage. |
| U2 L8 | operating profit | 营业利润 | Operating profit is profit from a company's usual business activities before taxes and before some financing effects are deducted; it is also called operating income. | 营业利润是公司通常业务活动产生的利润，在扣除税项以及某些融资影响之前计算；也称为营业收入。 | Focuses attention on the core business rather than one-off items. |
| U2 L8 | operating margin | 营业利润率 | Operating margin is a profitability ratio calculated as operating income or operating profit divided by revenue. | 营业利润率是一项盈利能力比率，计算方式为营业收入或营业利润除以收入。 | Helps students compare efficiency across companies or years. |
| U2 L8 | percentage-point difference | 百分点差异 | A percentage-point difference is the arithmetic difference between two percentages, not a percentage change. | 百分点差异是两个百分比之间的算术差额，而不是百分比变化。 | Prevents mistakes when comparing margins. |
| U2 L9 | operating cash flow | 经营现金流 | Operating cash flow is the net amount of cash provided by or used in a company's operating activities during a period. | 经营现金流是公司在某一期间经营活动产生或使用的现金净额。 | Separates cash generation from accounting profit. |
| U2 L9 | capital expenditure | 资本支出 | Capital expenditure is spending on physical or long-term fixed assets such as property, factories, equipment, technology infrastructure or stores. | 资本支出是用于实物资产或长期固定资产的支出，例如房产、工厂、设备、技术基础设施或门店。 | Explains why growing companies may need cash. |
| U2 L9 | free cash flow | 自由现金流 | Free cash flow is cash available to a company's investors after the company has made the investments needed to maintain itself as an ongoing business. | 自由现金流是公司为维持持续经营所需投资之后，可供公司投资者使用的现金。 | Introduces cash available after reinvestment needs. |
| U2 L10 | benchmark | 基准 | A benchmark is a standard or point of reference used to evaluate investment, portfolio or company performance. | 基准是用于评价投资、投资组合或公司表现的标准或参照点。 | Gives students a disciplined comparison point. |
| U2 L10 | trend | 趋势 | A trend is a longer-term pattern of movement in a particular direction, such as rising, falling or remaining broadly stable. | 趋势是朝某一方向运动的较长期模式，例如上升、下降或大体稳定。 | Moves analysis beyond one-year snapshots. |
| U2 L10 | scale | 规模 | Scale is the size of a company or activity, measured by figures such as revenue, users, stores, assets or market value. | 规模是公司或活动的大小，可用收入、用户数、门店数、资产或市值等数据衡量。 | Helps students compare large and small companies fairly. |

## Unit 3: Returns and valuation

| Ref | Term | Chinese support | Textbook definition | Chinese definition | Course use |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| U3 L11 | capital gain | 资本利得 | A capital gain is the profit made when an asset is sold for more than its purchase price. | 资本利得是资产以高于购买价格出售时获得的利润。 | Defines one way shareholders can make money. |
| U3 L11 | capital loss | 资本损失 | A capital loss is the loss made when an asset is sold for less than its purchase price. | 资本损失是资产以低于购买价格出售时发生的损失。 | Defines how price movement can hurt investors. |
| U3 L11 | return | 回报 | Return is the gain or loss earned from an investment over a stated holding period, including price change and any income received. | 回报是投资在某一持有期间获得的收益或损失，包括价格变动和收到的任何收入。 | Creates the basic measure of investment outcome. |
| U3 L12 | dividend | 股息 | A dividend is a distribution paid to shareholders based on the number of shares they own, usually in cash or additional shares. | 股息是根据股东所持股份数量向股东支付的分配，通常以现金或额外股票形式支付。 | Defines the income part of shareholder return. |
| U3 L12 | dividend yield | 股息收益率 | Dividend yield is the annual dividend per share divided by the share price and expressed as a percentage. | 股息收益率是每股年度股息除以股价后用百分比表示的指标。 | Lets students compare income relative to price. |
| U3 L12 | total return | 总回报 | Total return measures price appreciation or loss plus any income received from the investment over the period. | 总回报衡量某一期间内价格上涨或下跌加上投资收到的任何收入。 | Prevents students from looking only at price movement. |
| U3 L13 | market capitalisation | 市值 | Market capitalisation is the total market value of a company's equity, calculated as share price multiplied by shares outstanding. | 市值是公司股权的总市场价值，计算方式为股价乘以流通在外股份数。 | Shows how the market values the whole listed company. |
| U3 L13 | shares outstanding | 已发行股数 | Shares outstanding are the shares issued by a company and currently held by investors. | 已发行股数是公司已经发行且目前由投资者持有的股份。 | Supplies the second part of market-cap calculation. |
| U3 L13 | mega-cap | 超大市值公司 | A mega-cap company is a listed company with a very large market capitalisation compared with most companies in the market. | 超大市值公司是指相对于市场上多数公司而言具有非常大市值的上市公司。 | Gives students a size category for major listed firms. |
| U3 L14 | EPS | 每股收益 | EPS, or earnings per share, is the income earned during a period for each ordinary share, usually calculated as profit attributable to ordinary shareholders divided by the share count used in the calculation. | EPS，即每股收益，是某一期间每一普通股所赚取的收益，通常用归属于普通股股东的利润除以计算中使用的股份数量得出。 | Connects profit to one share. |
| U3 L14 | P/E ratio | 市盈率 | The P/E ratio is the ratio of share price to earnings per share, showing how much investors pay for each unit of current earnings. | 市盈率是股价与每股收益的比率，显示投资者愿意为每一单位当前收益支付多少价格。 | Introduces a simple valuation multiple. |
| U3 L14 | valuation | 估值 | Valuation is the process of estimating the value of an asset using variables related to future investment returns or comparisons with similar assets. | 估值是利用与未来投资回报有关的变量或与相似资产的比较来估计资产价值的过程。 | Turns price into an evidence-based judgement. |
| U3 L15 | cheap or expensive | 便宜或昂贵 | A share is cheap or expensive only in relation to evidence about value, earnings, growth, risk and expectations, not simply because the price per share is low or high. | 一只股票便宜或昂贵，只能相对于关于价值、收益、增长、风险和预期的证据来判断，而不能仅因为每股价格低或高。 | Breaks the low-price-equals-cheap misconception. |
| U3 L15 | expectations | 预期 | Expectations are assumptions about future performance, risk or growth that investors may already have reflected in the current price. | 预期是关于未来表现、风险或增长的假设，投资者可能已经把这些假设反映在当前价格中。 | Helps explain why good news can already be priced in. |
| U3 L15 | margin of safety | 安全边际 | Margin of safety is the room for error between the price paid and a cautious estimate of value. | 安全边际是支付价格与谨慎估计价值之间留出的误差空间。 | Gives students a risk-control idea for valuation. |

## Unit 4: Risk, portfolios and funds

| Ref | Term | Chinese support | Textbook definition | Chinese definition | Course use |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| U4 L16 | company-specific risk | 公司特定风险 | Company-specific risk is non-systematic risk tied to a particular company's operations, reputation, financial position, management or business environment. | 公司特定风险是与某一特定公司的经营、声誉、财务状况、管理或商业环境相关的非系统性风险。 | Focuses students on risks that affect one company. |
| U4 L16 | demand risk | 需求风险 | Demand risk is the risk that customers buy less than expected, reducing revenue, profit or growth. | 需求风险是客户购买少于预期，从而降低收入、利润或增长的风险。 | Links customer behaviour to future business results. |
| U4 L16 | execution risk | 执行风险 | Execution risk is the risk that a company fails to carry out its strategy, project or operational plan successfully. | 执行风险是公司未能成功实施其战略、项目或运营计划的风险。 | Shows why plans are not the same as results. |
| U4 L17 | sector | 行业 | A sector is a group of related industries or companies that share similar business activities or economic exposures. | 行业是由具有相似业务活动或经济暴露的相关产业或公司组成的群体。 | Creates a wider comparison group for company analysis. |
| U4 L17 | competition | 竞争 | Competition is rivalry between firms for customers, revenue, market share, talent, suppliers or profit. | 竞争是企业之间为了客户、收入、市场份额、人才、供应商或利润而进行的竞争。 | Explains why strong companies can still face pressure. |
| U4 L17 | consumer trend | 消费趋势 | A consumer trend is a change in what customers prefer, buy or avoid over time. | 消费趋势是客户偏好、购买或避免的内容随时间发生的变化。 | Links changing tastes to company risk. |
| U4 L18 | regulation | 监管 | Regulation is a system of government rules and enforcement that affects how companies, markets or investors may behave. | 监管是影响公司、市场或投资者行为的政府规则和执行体系。 | Introduces rules as a source of risk and discipline. |
| U4 L18 | compliance cost | 合规成本 | Compliance cost is the money, time and resources a company spends to obey laws, rules, standards and reporting requirements. | 合规成本是公司为遵守法律、规则、标准和报告要求而花费的金钱、时间和资源。 | Shows how regulation can reduce profit or change operations. |
| U4 L18 | policy risk | 政策风险 | Policy risk is the risk that changes in laws, regulation, taxation, subsidies or government priorities reduce future profit or change business prospects. | 政策风险是法律、监管、税收、补贴或政府优先事项变化降低未来利润或改变业务前景的风险。 | Helps students analyse government-related uncertainty. |
| U4 L19 | exchange rate | 汇率 | An exchange rate is the price of one currency expressed in terms of another currency. | 汇率是一种货币以另一种货币表示的价格，用来说明两种货币之间的兑换关系。 | Gives the currency basis for global company analysis. |
| U4 L19 | export exposure | 出口敞口 | Export exposure is a company's reliance on sales to customers in other countries or currencies. | 出口敞口是公司对其他国家或其他货币客户销售的依赖程度。 | Links revenue geography to currency and demand risk. |
| U4 L19 | translation effect | 折算影响 | A translation effect is the change in reported financial figures caused by converting foreign-currency results into the company's reporting currency. | 折算影响是把外币业绩换算为公司报告货币时，所导致的报告财务数据变化。 | Explains why accounts can move when currencies move. |
| U4 L20 | ETF | 交易型开放式指数基金 | An ETF is an exchange-traded fund, a pooled investment fund that holds a basket of assets and trades on a stock exchange like a share. | ETF 是交易型开放式指数基金，一种持有一篮子资产、并像股票一样在证券交易所交易的集合投资基金。 | Introduces funds as an alternative to single shares. |
| U4 L20 | index fund | 指数基金 | An index fund is a fund designed to track the performance of a market index rather than choose individual securities through active stock-picking. | 指数基金是旨在跟踪某一市场指数表现的基金，而不是通过主动选股来选择单个证券。 | Shows passive diversification. |
| U4 L20 | diversification | 分散化 | Diversification is spreading investment across different holdings, sectors, regions or asset types to reduce dependence on any single source of risk. | 分散化是把投资分布在不同持仓、行业、地区或资产类型中，以降低对任何单一风险来源的依赖。 | Teaches the main reason one fund can hold many companies. |
| U4 L20 | portfolio weight | 投资组合权重 | Portfolio weight is the value of one holding divided by the total value of the portfolio and expressed as a percentage. | 投资组合权重是一项持仓价值除以投资组合总价值并用百分比表示的比例。 | Measures concentration in one holding. |
