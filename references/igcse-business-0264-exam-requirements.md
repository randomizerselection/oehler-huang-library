## Assessment Overview

| Paper | Duration | Marks | Weighting | Structure | Unit 5 lesson implication |
| --- | --- | ---: | ---: | --- | --- |
| Paper 1 | 1 hour 30 minutes | 80 | 50% | Four compulsory short-answer/data-response questions. Specimen questions use 2, 4, 6 and 8-mark parts. | Train precise terms, short applied points, developed explanation and 8-mark judgement. |
| Paper 2 | 1 hour 30 minutes | 80 | 50% | Four compulsory questions based on one case-study insert with data, tables, graphs, extracts or advertisements. Specimen questions use applied 8-mark part (a) tasks and 12-mark level-response decision tasks. | Train case reading, data selection, applied calculations and justified recommendations that reject alternatives. |

Candidates should use the mark allocation to judge the detail and length required. Lesson exam tasks should show marks visibly and should not ask for generic business advice without case evidence.

## Assessment Objectives

| AO | Focus | Overall weighting | Paper 1 | Paper 2 | Teaching consequence |
| --- | --- | ---: | ---: | ---: | --- |
| AO1 | Knowledge and understanding of business terms, concepts and theories | 35% | 40% | 30% | Teach accurate definitions and named concepts. |
| AO2 | Application to a given business | 30% | 30% | 30% | Require a distinct contextual reference in each developed point. |
| AO3 | Analysis of business data and information | 22% | 20% | 25% | Build consequence chains from case evidence and figures. |
| AO4 | Justified decisions, conclusions and recommendations based on evidence | 13% | 10% | 15% | Require a final judgement, not a summary. |

Application is not an optional add-on. It is 30% overall and 30% on both papers, so every Unit 5 practice sequence should force use of the given business, not just recall of a finance term.

## Command Words

| Command | Exam meaning | Classroom trigger |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Calculate | Work out from facts, figures or information. | Show method and final figure. |
| Consider | Review and respond to given information. | Use evidence for more than one option before deciding. |
| Define | Give precise meaning. | State the meaning, not only an example. |
| Explain | Set out reasons or relationships and support with relevant evidence. | Term -> case evidence -> consequence. |
| Identify | Name, select or recognise. | Give the required number of distinct points. |
| Justify | Support a case with evidence or argument. | Decide and prove why the choice fits better than alternatives. |
| Outline | Set out the main points. | Name the point and attach it to the case. |
| State | Express in clear terms. | Short, direct answer. |

## Marking Templates

### Define [2]

- 2 marks: clear, precise understanding of the business term.
- 1 mark: partial understanding.
- Examples alone are not enough if they do not define the term.

Student routine: `Term means...` then only add an example if it clarifies the meaning.

### Identify / State

- Award one mark for each valid point up to the maximum.
- If the question asks for two points, train students to write two distinct points only.
- Avoid repeats, vague synonyms or answers that shift the question focus.

Student routine: `Point 1... Point 2...`

### Outline [4]

- Specimen Paper 1 outline items award one mark for identification and one mark for relevant application per point.
- Application must make sense in relation to the point being made.

Student routine for each point: `Business term or point -> case reference.`

### Explain [6]

- Specimen Paper 1 explain items award one mark for identification, one mark for relevant application and one mark for relevant development per point.
- For two-point explain questions, each point should use different case evidence and a different consequence.

Student routine for each point: `Business term -> case evidence -> business consequence.`

### Paper 1 Justify [8]

- Specimen Paper 1 8-mark questions award up to 2 marks each for knowledge, application, analysis and evaluation.
- Evaluation must be a justified decision that follows the analysis.
- Do not repeat the same point as both knowledge and analysis.

Student routine: `Option/issue -> applied benefit -> applied drawback or limit -> justified decision.`

### Paper 2 Applied Explain [8]

- Specimen Paper 2 part (a) tasks usually reward applied explanation across several required points.
- Application must be tied to the case study, not copied as a decorative phrase.
- For calculations, show a method when the mark scheme rewards it, then use the figure in interpretation.

Student routine: `Point -> case evidence or data -> consequence for this business.`

### Paper 2 Consider / Justify [12]

- Level 1: limited knowledge in context, simple judgement or outline.
- Level 2: detailed discussion of at least one option in context, with some judgement.
- Level 3: detailed discussion of two or more options in context, with a well-justified recommendation or conclusion.
- Top-band responses explain why the rejected alternative is less suitable.

Student routine: `Option A chain -> option B chain -> recommendation -> rejected alternative.`

## Secondary 0450 Examiner Habits

Use these only where they support the 0264 specimen approach:

- Definitions need to be precise; examples do not replace the meaning.
- Each developed point needs distinct application. Reusing the same case phrase for multiple points is weak.
- Analysis should develop the point and show how or why it matters to the business. Do not restate the knowledge point.
- Developed reasoning scores better than lists.
- A recommendation or conclusion must clearly answer the question, use earlier analysis and explain why an alternative is weaker.
- Read the exact question focus. Misreading the command or business situation loses marks even when the business theory is correct.

## Unit 5 Exam Implications

- Use Harbor Phone Repair as a teaching case only. Label it as original classroom practice, not as a Cambridge case.
- Every Unit 5 deck should include at least one Paper 1 and one Paper 2 exam-practice item.
- Finance-needs answers must connect the need to purpose and time period: start-up capital, expansion, replacement of non-current assets, new technology or working capital.
- Source-of-finance recommendations must use amount, purpose, time period, cost, legal form, control and existing borrowing.
- Cash-flow tasks should train calculation method, interpretation of negative balances and short-term solutions matched to the cause and timing of the problem.
- Model answers should visibly train this chain: business term -> case evidence -> consequence -> judgement or rejected alternative.
