# Official Stock Market Game resource archive

Accessed: 15 July 2026  
Publisher: SIFMA Foundation  
Scope: public, official resources useful for the Grade 9 Investment and Financial Decision-Making course and its required, full-academic-year Stock Market Game laboratory.

Course use: SMG is the recurring application and assessment environment in all fifty lessons. These official materials support the lesson-specific actions in the canonical course map; they do not form a separate parallel curriculum or replace the course's goal-first investment concepts.

## Verified official findings used in the syllabus

- SMG is a team-based virtual investment simulation for students in grades 4-12. The official Start Here guide recommends three to five students per portfolio and defined team roles.
- Advisors and students must understand the national rules and any local program rules. Local dates, fees, awards and additional rules vary by coordinator or program.
- The official lesson sequence is `Understanding SMG`, `Before You Invest`, `Selecting Your Investments`, `Tracking Your Investments` and `Reflections`.
- SMG encourages first trades during `Selecting Your Investments`, after students have learned the program, companies, stocks, ticker symbols and quotes.
- Under the national rules, a team does not receive a portfolio record or appear in rankings until its first transaction is entered successfully. The rankings help page describes the qualifying action as at least one completed buy or short-sell transaction.
- National rules require stock and mutual-fund buy orders and stock short-sell orders to contain at least 10 shares. A student may be assigned to only one portfolio per game period.
- National rules permit margin and short selling in some account types, but this course applies stricter local classroom rules: long-only, no margin, no short selling, and no order without an approved evidence record.
- Ranking may use total equity or performance relative to the S&P 500, depending on the coordinator. Course grades therefore assess evidence, reasoning, role fulfilment and reflection rather than rank or return.
