How the course works
Real companies Every lesson starts from one listed company.
Built step by step Terms, definitions and formulas accumulate across lessons.
Careful judgement You learn evidence before opinion. This is not short-term stock speculation or personal investment advice.
Student overview

Six units, thirty taught lessons

Each 40-minute lesson has a company example, one analyst question, a clear knowledge target and a short check. Short one-class team tasks appear when useful; assessment checks stay small and contained.

Before the first share

Start with investment analysis

Students first learn what the course is about: using evidence to study companies, shares, possible return, risk and price. It is not a course about short-term stock speculation, stock tips, market timing or personal buy/sell advice.

Foundation exercise

Meet the first share-price graph

Students observe Tencent as a familiar company, separate the company from one listed share, and read a price graph before doing formal calculations later.

Lessons 1-5

Market foundations

Investment-analysis purpose, shares, share prices, exchanges, quote pages, source discipline and the first price-movement chain.

Lessons 6-10

Financial statements

Revenue first, then direct costs, gross margin, operating margin, cash flow and fair comparison.

Lessons 11-15

Returns and valuation

Capital gains, dividends, market value, EPS, P/E, valuation risk and the basic risk-return trade-off.

Lessons 16-20

Risk, portfolios and funds

Company, sector, regulation, currency, ETF/index-fund and concentration risks.

Lessons 21-25

Sector case labs

Platform, manufacturing, financial, consumer-brand and global comparison cases.

Lessons 26-30

Synthesis case labs

Recovery, operating leverage, app monetisation, debt risk and final quality-price-risk judgement.

Lesson 1Tencent

What is investment analysis, and what is a share?

什么是投资分析，什么是股票？

Focus: what the course studies, why evidence comes before opinion, why this is not short-term stock speculation, how a company is different from one listed share, and how a share-price graph gives an analyst a first question rather than an answer.

  • investment analysis 投资分析 - using evidence to study possible return, risk and price before making a judgement.

Evidence: Anta Sports revenue, margin and brand or inventory notes.

Check: Complete one brand-to-margin evidence map with one limitation.

  • Builds on: revenue, margin and competition analysis from Lessons 6-8 and 17.
  • New learning: brand equity can support pricing power and margins, but inventory risk and changing demand can weaken the case.
  • Avoid overlap: do not repeat generic sector-risk language unless it is tied to brand, inventory or pricing power.
  • Common misconception: a strong or famous brand guarantees high profit and low risk.
  • Evidence task: use Anta revenue, margin, brand and inventory notes to classify support and limitation evidence.
  • Student output: one brand-to-margin evidence map plus one limitation linked to inventory or demand.
Lesson 25Nike

How can we compare a global brand with a China/HK brand?

如何比较全球品牌与中国/香港品牌？

Focus: comparable-company analysis across markets.

  • multinational 跨国公司 - a company operating across several countries.
  • comparable company 可比公司 - a similar firm used for comparison.
  • local/global risk 本地/全球风险 - risk from specific local markets or worldwide exposure.

Formula: compare two companies using the same metric set.

Evidence: Nike figures plus one China/HK brand comparison using aligned metrics.

Check: Submit one comparable-company alignment row and one fair comparison sentence.

  • Builds on: comparison discipline from Lesson 10 and brand analysis from Lesson 24.
  • New learning: comparable-company analysis requires aligned metrics, time periods, source quality and local/global risk awareness.
  • Avoid overlap: do not introduce new financial metrics; focus on fair comparison method.
  • Common misconception: two brands can be compared fairly even when the figures use different definitions, years or currencies.
  • Evidence task: align Nike data with one China/HK brand comparison using the same metric set.
  • Student output: one comparable-company alignment row plus one fair comparison paragraph naming metric, source date and limitation.
Lesson 26Haidilao

Can a restaurant company recover after a difficult period?

餐饮公司能否在困难时期后恢复？

Focus: turnaround evidence and store-network quality.

  • turnaround 经营复苏 - improvement after a weak period.
  • store network 门店网络 - the set of locations through which a company serves customers.
  • same-store sales 同店销售 - sales from stores open in both comparison periods.

Formula: revenue per store = total revenue / number of stores, if data allows.

Evidence: Haidilao store count, revenue or operating recovery evidence.

Check: Give a red/amber/green recovery verdict and justify the evidence gap.

  • Builds on: evidence discipline, revenue analysis, margin caution and valuation/risk judgement from earlier units.
  • New learning: turnaround analysis asks whether weak performance is genuinely improving and whether the store network is higher quality.
  • Avoid overlap: do not introduce full restaurant accounting; revenue per store is optional and only if the data is clean.
  • Common misconception: one recovery figure proves the turnaround is complete.
  • Evidence task: compare Haidilao store count, revenue or operating recovery evidence across periods.
  • Student output: one red/amber/green recovery verdict explaining whether the claim needs more evidence.
Lesson 27Trip.com

Why can travel companies rise and fall with the economy?

旅游公司为什么会随经济周期起落？

Focus: cyclical demand and operating leverage.

  • cyclical demand 周期性需求 - demand that rises and falls with economic conditions.
  • recovery 复苏 - return toward earlier activity or profit levels.
  • operating leverage 经营杠杆 - profit sensitivity when fixed costs are high.

Formula: operating margin = operating profit / revenue x 100.

Evidence: Trip.com revenue and operating profit before and after a travel cycle change.

Check: Complete a demand -> revenue -> operating profit -> margin chain diagram.

  • Builds on: margin analysis from Unit 2, sector/cyclical risk from Unit 4 and turnaround thinking from Lesson 26.
  • New learning: cyclical demand and operating leverage can make profit move faster than revenue when fixed costs are important.
  • Avoid overlap: do not turn the lesson into macroeconomics; keep the focus on company evidence before and after a travel cycle change.
  • Common misconception: revenue and profit should rise or fall at the same rate.
  • Evidence task: compare Trip.com revenue and operating profit before and after a travel-cycle shift.
  • Student output: one operating-leverage chain diagram linking travel demand, revenue, operating profit and margin.
Lesson 28Kuaishou

How does a free app turn users into revenue?

免费应用如何把用户转化为收入？

Focus: monetisation and user metrics.

  • active users 活跃用户 - people who use a service during a measured period.
  • monetisation 商业化变现 - turning user activity into revenue.
  • ARPU 每用户平均收入 - average revenue per user.

Formula: ARPU = revenue / active users.

Evidence: Kuaishou user number and revenue data from one reporting period.

Check: Complete one monetisation ladder and one ARPU limitation sentence.

  • Builds on: platform thinking from Lesson 21 and revenue/margin caution from Unit 2.
  • New learning: user metrics must be connected to monetisation; ARPU shows average revenue per active user, not profit per user.
  • Avoid overlap: do not repeat all platform-business concepts; focus on the bridge from users to revenue.
  • Common misconception: more users automatically mean more revenue, higher ARPU or a stronger investment case.
  • Evidence task: read Kuaishou active-user and revenue data from one reporting period.
  • Student output: one monetisation ladder plus ARPU calculation and limitation sentence about users, revenue and profit.
Lesson 29Lenovo

When does borrowing become a risk for shareholders?

借款什么时候会成为股东风险？

Focus: debt, liabilities and leverage risk.

  • debt 债务 - money a company has borrowed and must repay.
  • liabilities 负债 - obligations a company owes to others.
  • gearing or leverage 负债杠杆 - the use of debt in a company's financing.

Formula: debt-to-equity = total debt / equity, if the data is suitable.

Evidence: Lenovo balance-sheet extract with debt, liabilities or financing notes.

Check: Write one leverage benefit-risk note.

  • Builds on: risk-evidence-effect chains and earlier evidence reading of company reports.
  • New learning: debt can fund growth but adds obligations; leverage raises both potential benefit and shareholder risk.
  • Avoid overlap: keep advanced solvency and interest-coverage ratios optional; use debt-to-equity only if the data is suitable for Grade 9.
  • Common misconception: borrowing is always bad, or borrowing is harmless if the company is large.
  • Evidence task: read a Lenovo balance-sheet extract and identify debt, liabilities, equity or financing notes.
  • Student output: one leverage benefit-risk note with a simple ratio only if the figures are clear.
Lesson 30Costco

Can a steady business still be a bad investment at the wrong price?

稳定企业在价格错误时仍可能是差投资吗？

Focus: quality, defensiveness and final valuation synthesis.

  • defensive business 防御型业务 - a business whose demand may be more stable in difficult times.
  • quality company 优质公司 - a company with strong operations, brand, margins or resilience.
  • valuation risk 估值风险 - risk that the price already assumes too much good news.

Formula: synthesis of growth, margin, P/E and risk; no new formula.

Evidence: Costco growth, margin and valuation evidence from dated sources.

Check: Write a final quality-price-risk memo that avoids speculation, balances possible return with risk and gives no personal investment advice.

  • Builds on: the full course: source discipline, growth, margin, cash, returns, valuation, risk, funds and case comparison.
  • New learning: defensive and quality businesses still need valuation discipline because price can already assume good news; rational investment analysis must balance possible return with risk and reject speculation or tips.
  • Avoid overlap: do not add a new formula; this is the final synthesis lesson.
  • Common misconception: a steady business is automatically a safe or good investment.
  • Evidence task: combine Costco growth, margin, valuation and risk evidence from dated sources.
  • Student output: a final quality-price-risk memo using dated evidence, possible return, price paid and risk without speculation or personal investment advice.

Course standards

How lessons are built

These rules keep the lessons focused, fair and evidence-based as new decks, quizzes and data snapshots are added.

Lesson focus

One clear decision

Each lesson starts with the company and analyst question, then builds toward one evidence task, one knowledge target and one short written judgement.

Evidence rules

Use dated evidence

Every data point should show the company, stock code or listing, source title, source URL, publication date, accessed date, key figures and what the evidence can and cannot prove.

Language support

Key terms get support

English stays as the main teaching language, with Chinese support for key terms, objectives, difficult prompts and formula wording.

Practice checks

Check knowledge before judgement

You practise definitions, quick calculations, quote-page reading, evidence selection, risk notes and short explain/evaluate answers.

Short team tasks

Finish together in one class

Use quick collaboration for scenario sorting, quote-page bid/ask labs, ETF vs single-stock comparisons and company-risk debates. Each task ends with an individual written check.
