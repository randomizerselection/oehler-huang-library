Investment and Financial Decision-Making

Personal Wealth, Markets and Analysis

This two-semester course covers investment goals, asset classes, stock markets, company analysis, portfolio management, family investment decisions and finance careers.

The SIFMA Foundation's Stock Market Game is the required course laboratory: every lesson applies its concept to the team's policy, watchlist, portfolio, transactions, evidence or benchmark.

Students work with fictional cases and dated evidence. The private Unit 1 Passport uses broad personal goal bands, never family financial figures. The course does not recommend securities or provide personal financial advice.

Course structure

Six units, fifty taught lessons

The sequence moves from family goals to investor profiles, investment choices, market understanding, company analysis, portfolio decisions and a final family investment strategy.

Lessons 1-8

Personal Investment Foundations

Goals, time horizon, saving and investing, compounding, inflation, risk-return, risk capacity and a written investment plan.

Unit output: Team SMG investment policy and individual decision charter.

Open the eight-page My Future Investor Passport

Lessons 9-17

Investment Choices for Families

Cash, deposits, bonds, shares, funds, index and active approaches, fees, diversification and investing methods.

Unit output: SMG portfolio-construction and investment-choice memo.

Lessons 18-26

How Markets Work

Share issuance, primary and secondary markets, trade completion, quotes, price changes, return, indices and market news.

Unit output: SMG transaction, quote, return and benchmark memo.

Lessons 27-35

Analysing Companies

Analyst ethics, business models, financial statements, peer comparison, qualitative risk, valuation and memo writing.

Unit output: Company-analysis memo on an SMG holding or candidate.

Lessons 36-43

Portfolios and Investor Behaviour

Asset allocation, position size, geographic and currency exposure, remaining risk, comparison, rebalancing, monitoring and behavioural mistakes.

Unit output: SMG portfolio review and rebalance decision.

Lessons 44-50

Family Investment Decisions and Careers

Education, housing, retirement, windfalls, unsafe offers, investment careers and a complete family strategy.

Unit output: Final SMG evaluation, family strategy and team presentation.

Course outcomes

Course outcomes

By the end of the course, students should be able to complete the following tasks.

  • Define an investment goal, time horizon, liquidity need and risk limits.
  • Compare cash, bonds, shares and funds by ownership, return, risk, cost and suitability.
  • Explain how exchanges, brokers, prices, indices and market information work.
  • Use business, financial-statement, risk and valuation evidence in a company-analysis memo.
  • Construct and review a simple portfolio for a fictional family case.
  • Use a shared Stock Market Game portfolio as evidence in every lesson, from the first investment policy to the final benchmark-aware defence.
  • Describe common investment roles, professional responsibilities and ethical limits.
Core course laboratory

Every student participates in The Stock Market Game

The registered 2026-2027 full-academic-year session begins with the course on 1 September 2026. The exact platform window and any additional rules must be confirmed in the advisor account because official registration guidance says they vary by program.

Every student

Required course participation

Every student belongs to exactly one team, completes the rules check, rotates roles and contributes to the SMG lab in every lesson. Individual exit judgements are curated into six unit outputs and a final reflection.

Platform minimum

One transaction is not enough

Nationally, a team enters rankings only after at least one successful buy or short-sell transaction. After the course's initial qualifying purchase there is no trade quota: a justified hold or no-trade decision counts equally.

Launch gate

First trade after Lesson 8

After the live rules, rules quiz, official company-stock-ticker-quote checks, team plan and research note are approved, each team completes one successful long stock buy of at least 10 shares at the transition into Unit 2. Every student records their contribution to the proposal.

Course safeguards

Long-only, evidence before action

No margin, borrowing or short selling. No order without dated evidence, a named proposal author, a team reason and a review trigger. The stricter of national, local and course rules governs.

Every lesson

SMG is the main application

Roughly 35-50% of each lesson uses a frozen portfolio, watchlist, transaction, quote or benchmark snapshot. The SMG lab replaces compatible generic practice rather than being added as a second activity.

Assessment

Process, not rank or return

All six unit outputs are built from the SMG evidence trail. Grades reward research quality, concept use, reasoning, collaboration, monitoring, rule compliance and reflection; rank and raw return carry no marks.

Course alignment

Six phases across all fifty lessons

The official SMG sequence and the course concept sequence operate as a dual spine: investment ideas supply the method, and the live team portfolio supplies the recurring decision environment.

Assessment evidence

One evidence trail, six assessed outputs

Each lesson adds one concise team evidence row and one individual exit judgement. Students then curate—not reproduce—that work for the unit assessment.

Course boundariesScope, advice limits and language support
Course standards

Boundaries that keep the course useful

Personal relevance comes from family investment decisions, not from turning the course into general household administration.

In scope

Personal wealth decisions

Goals, horizons, risk, investment choices, markets, companies, portfolios, family cases and career foundations.

Out of scope

General consumer finance

No budgeting, payslips, consumer credit, insurance administration, tax administration or general banking units.

No-advice boundary

Teach method, not tips

Students may judge mock cases but do not recommend named products or tell real people what to buy, sell or hold.

Language support

English with concise Chinese support

Important questions, terms, definitions, tasks and model conclusions receive Simplified Chinese support.
