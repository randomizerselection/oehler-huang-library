# Stock Market Game course integration guide

Course: Investment and Financial Decision-Making  
Session: registered 2026-2027 full academic year  
Course start: 1 September 2026  
Participation: required for every enrolled student

This is the teacher-facing operating guide. The canonical machine-readable contract remains [`course-map-financial-decisions-data.js`](course-map-financial-decisions-data.js); official documents and live-source links are catalogued in [`references/stock-market-game/README.md`](references/stock-market-game/README.md).

## Core-course operating model

- The Stock Market Game is the course laboratory and assessment spine. Every lesson has a required, concept-specific SMG application.
- Allocate roughly 35-50% of each lesson to the SMG core lab. Scale the exact minutes to the timetable and give the fourteen milestone lessons the larger share.
- The SMG lab replaces compatible generic case practice. It is not an additional activity or a second homework course.
- Each lesson adds one concise team evidence row and one individual exit judgement. The six unit assessments curate this evidence rather than asking students to reproduce it.
- Fourteen lessons are summative SMG milestones; the other thirty-six are required formative labs that build the same year-long portfolio evidence trail.
- After the required first qualifying purchase, there is no trading quota. Hold and no-trade decisions receive equal credit when supported by evidence.
- Portfolio rank, raw return and a single winning trade carry no marks.

## Scalable lesson architecture

Use proportions rather than fixed minutes so the model works with different timetables.

1. **First judgement and retrieval - about 20%:** start with the lesson question and an initial portfolio judgement.
2. **Focused teaching - about 20%:** teach the one concept students need to improve that judgement.
3. **SMG core lab - about 40%:** apply the concept to the team's policy, watchlist, portfolio, transaction, quote, evidence or benchmark.
4. **Decision and exit - about 20%:** record one team evidence row and one individual exit judgement; enter an order only when the evidence supports it.

Capture the required portfolio, watchlist, transaction, quote or benchmark evidence at the start of the lesson. Use that frozen snapshot for teaching and assessment so live price changes do not destabilise the task.

## Before the course starts

1. Confirm the exact session dates and local rules in the advisor account.
2. Create teams of three to five where class numbers permit and assign every student to exactly one portfolio.
3. Keep an advisor-only roster containing team IDs and student names. Do not place passwords in student journals, handouts or shared documents.
4. Decide how three- or four-person teams will combine the five official roles.
5. Prepare one shared year-long evidence log per team and an individual exit-judgement record that can be curated into six unit checkpoints.
6. Open and test one team account before students begin.
7. Print or share the [national rules handout](references/stock-market-game/rules-and-platform/SMG_National_Rules_Handout.pdf), [rules quiz](references/stock-market-game/rules-and-platform/SMG_Rules_Quiz.pdf), [team roles](references/stock-market-game/rules-and-platform/SMG_Team_Roles.pdf) and [advisor guide](references/stock-market-game/program-guides/SMG_Advisor_Guide_2022.pdf).

## Launch sequence

| Course point | Required action | Evidence |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Lesson 1 | Form teams, assign roles and open logs | Roster and first role record |
| Lesson 3 | Read the live national and local rules; complete the rules quiz | Quiz and participation agreement |
| Lesson 7 | Complete official company, stock, ticker-symbol and quote checks; research one eligible candidate without trading | Candidate research note |
| Lesson 8 | Approve the team investment policy and first proposal | Teacher-approved plan and proposal |
| Unit 2 transition | Enter one teacher-approved long stock buy of at least 10 shares, subject to stricter local rules | Executed transaction status and each student's contribution note |

Until the launch gate is complete, teams may research and build a watchlist but may not enter an order.

## Every-lesson SMG lab

Use the lesson-specific SMG action in the canonical course map. The action must apply that day's concept to the team's actual learning environment; do not substitute a generic portfolio check.

1. Capture the lesson's required portfolio, watchlist, transaction, quote, evidence or benchmark snapshot.
2. Inspect the evidence through the lesson concept and identify what the team still does not know.
3. Complete the named lesson action and choose `hold`, `research`, `buy`, `sell`, `rebalance` or `no trade` when a portfolio decision is required.
4. Check any decision against the team policy, national rules, local rules and course safeguards.
5. Add one concise team evidence row with the responsible student, limitation and review trigger.
6. Every student completes the lesson's individual exit judgement. If an order is entered, check whether it is pending, executed or rejected; submission is not execution.

### Team decision log

| Field | Required entry |
| --- | --- |
| Decision date | Date the evidence was reviewed |
| Student and role | Responsible student and current team role |
| Dated evidence | Source, date, material figure or claim, and one limitation |
| Proposed action | Hold, research, buy, sell, rebalance or no trade |
| Plan and risk fit | Goal, horizon, risk limit and course-rule check |
| Team decision | Final decision and any material dissent |
| Order checker | Student who checked the order; use a different student from the proposal author where team size permits |
| Platform result | Not entered, pending, executed or rejected |
| Review trigger | Date, evidence or threshold that requires reconsideration |

## Role rotation

Rotate roles every two course weeks.

| Role | Core responsibility | Evidence of participation |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Director | Run the decision meeting and ensure each voice is heard | Meeting decision and dissent record |
| Researcher | Find and date the evidence | Source, material evidence and limitation |
| Portfolio Coordinator | Check holdings, cash, weights and transaction history | Portfolio-status check |
| Data Entry Coordinator | Enter an approved order and check status | Pending, executed or rejected record |
| Lead Reporter | Complete the team evidence row and assign the review trigger | Submitted lesson evidence row |

Teams with fewer than five students may combine roles. The proposal author and order checker should be different students where possible. Passwords remain private regardless of role rotation.

## Six assessed SMG outputs

| Unit | Team evidence | Individual evidence | Existing assessment |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1. Foundations | Roster, roles, rules, goal-horizon-risk rules, policy and first proposal | Curate the strongest exit judgements into a personal decision charter | Team SMG investment policy and individual decision charter |
| 2. Investment choices | Cash rule, asset comparisons, cost check, diversification audit and fit review | Defend one chosen criterion and one rejected alternative | SMG portfolio-construction and investment-choice memo |
| 3. Markets | Issuance note, transaction trace, quote annotation, price explanation, return and benchmark | Explain one transaction, price or return judgement with a limitation | SMG transaction, quote, return and benchmark memo |
| 4. Companies | Ethics, business model, statements, peers, risks and valuation for a holding or candidate | Author or critically review one balanced company judgement | Junior company-analysis memo on an SMG holding or candidate |
| 5. Portfolios | Weights, concentration, exposures, remaining risk, alternatives, rebalance, dashboard and bias | Defend the rebalance or no-rebalance decision and its review trigger | SMG portfolio review and evidence-based rebalance decision |
| 6. Reflection | Goal stress tests, windfall decision, rules re-check, careers and final portfolio defence | Explain contribution, changed judgement and one process improvement | Final SMG evaluation, family strategy and team presentation |

InvestWrite may be offered as a separate extension when eligibility and timing fit, but it is not required for course completion.

## Trade approval gate

Approve an order only when all of the following are true:

- the security is eligible under the live national and local rules;
- the proposal fits the team goal, horizon, risk limits and long-only course policy;
- the evidence is dated and includes a material fact plus a limitation;
- the team has considered a hold or no-trade alternative;
- the proposal author and order checker are recorded;
- the team has stated a review trigger;
- sufficient cash is available without margin or borrowing.

Reject or postpone an order when evidence is missing, the case depends on price excitement, the position would breach the team's risk limits, or the platform status cannot be checked.

## Fourteen required milestones

The canonical map marks Lessons 1, 3, 7, 8, 15, 17, 21, 25, 35, 36, 41, 43, 48 and 50 as summative SMG milestones. Every other lesson has a required formative SMG core lab; these are not optional connection points. All fifty actions build one portfolio evidence trail, so the milestone and unit outputs should reuse the work rather than create duplicate logs or homework.
