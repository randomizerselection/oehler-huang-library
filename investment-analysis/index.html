Syllabus overview

Investment Analysis: Evidence-Based Investing

投资分析：基于证据的投资

A 50-lesson, two-semester course where students analyse companies, funds, bonds, diversification, investor fit, behaviour and final memo writing.

Decision-first syllabus overview

The standard route teaches students to improve a first classroom judgement with one key idea, one evidence task and one justified next action. Cases are embedded throughout the course, from Tencent and HKEX to funds, bonds, investor profiles and portfolio memos.

  • Know what you are buying. Name the company, listing, stock code, fund, bond, cash product or portfolio exposure.
  • Check whether it fits the investor. Separate goals, time horizon, liquidity needs and risk level before judging possible return.
  • Read investment evidence. Use dated figures and state what each source can and cannot prove.
  • Compare return, risk, price and cost. Ask whether the case is attractive, risky, too expensive, too costly or incomplete.
  • Choose the next action. Write a defensible classroom action: consider, watch, avoid, compare with another choice or gather more evidence.
Semester 1 Lessons 1-30

Foundations, market access, company evidence, statements, return, valuation and investor fit.

Semester 2 Lessons 31-50

Funds, bonds, costs, diversification, behaviour, portfolio monitoring, comparison and final memo.

课程概览

50 节课训练学生从第一印象走向有证据的投资判断。第一学期 30 节课和三次考试，第二学期 20 节课和两次考试。

课堂任务是分析证据和写出下一步行动，不提供股票推荐、市场择时或个人投资建议。
