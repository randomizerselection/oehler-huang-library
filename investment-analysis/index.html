Investment and Financial Decision-Making

Learn how goals, risk, markets, companies and portfolios shape evidence-based investment decisions for families.

Students use fictional cases and dated sources. The private Unit 1 Passport uses broad personal goal bands, never family financial figures. The course does not recommend securities or provide personal financial advice.

Full course route

Six units over two semesters

The course moves from personal goals and investment choices to markets, company analysis, portfolios and final family decisions.

  1. 01

    Personal Investment Foundations

    Goals, time horizons, compounding, inflation, risk and an investment plan.

  2. 02

    Investment Choices for Families

    Cash, bonds, shares, funds, fees, diversification and investing methods.

  3. 03

    How Markets Work

    Issuance, exchanges, trading, prices, returns, indices and market news.

  4. 04

    Analysing Companies

    Business models, financial statements, comparison, risk and valuation.

  5. 05

    Portfolios and Investor Behaviour

    Allocation, concentration, currency, rebalancing, monitoring and bias.

  6. 06

    Family Investment Decisions and Careers

    Education, housing, retirement, unsafe offers, professional roles and the final strategy.
